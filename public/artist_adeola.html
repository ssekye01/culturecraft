Artist Profile

Adeola Adebayo

Yoruba Patterns of the Ancestors • Painting, sculpture & mixed media

Background

Adeola grew up between Lagos and Ibadan, where festival processions, talking drums, and beadwork were part of everyday life. Trained in fine art, she combines classical painting with design details drawn from Yoruba regalia and architecture. Her early exposure to artisans in local markets shaped her sense of color and texture, and she often credits her grandmother’s storytelling for inspiring her focus on cultural memory. Today, Adeola works at the intersection of tradition and contemporary design, using her practice to preserve and reinterpret Yoruba visual language for a global audience.



What inspires this collection

The Yoruba collection is Adeola’s love letter to ceremony and gathering. Pieces like the Oshun River Goddess Canvas, Market Day in Ibadan, and the Yoruba Drum Circle Night Scene capture the warmth of shared music, storytelling, and spiritual spaces. Repeating motifs — cowrie shells, double axes, beaded crowns — are her way of honoring elders’ stories while presenting them in a contemporary, gallery-ready way. Adeola also draws inspiration from natural rhythms, especially the movement of water and firelight, which she sees as symbols of guidance and renewal. Together, these elements form a collection that celebrates resilience, joy, and the continuity of Yoruba heritage.
