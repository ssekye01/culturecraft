Artist Profile

Julien Moreau

Parisian Heritage Collection • Painting, artifacts & mixed media

Background

Julien Moreau grew up between Paris and the small coastal town of Saint-Malo, splitting his childhood between zinc rooftops and windy Atlantic cliffs. His parents ran a corner café near Montmartre, and he spent slow afternoons sketching customers, chipped café bowls, and the changing light over the rooftops outside. Summers were for visiting grandparents in Provence and Brittany, where sunflowers, lavender fields, fishing boats, and lighthouses quietly worked their way into his sketchbooks. Trained in illustration and ceramics, Julien is drawn to objects that feel both lived-in and timeless—like something you might inherit from a grandparent’s kitchen or discover in a flea market. Today, he uses soft, nostalgic color palettes and gentle, storybook details to capture everyday scenes of French life in a way that feels intimate and welcoming.



What inspires this collection

The Parisian Heritage collection is Julien’s tribute to the small moments that define how he experiences France—morning coffee in a café bol, twilight walks along the Seine, and salty breezes on the Breton coast. Pieces like the Paris Rooftops Evening Canvas Print and the Paris Rooftop Nightlight echo the view from his parents’ apartment, where chimneys, glowing windows, and the distant Eiffel Tower created a nightly skyline of warm lights and cool slate blues. Works such as the Café au Lait Bol Set and Parisian Café Espresso Set celebrate the ritual of breakfast in a neighborhood café, while the Côte d’Azur Coastal Print Tray and Brittany Coastal Ceramic Plate recall summers spent between bright umbrellas, sailboats, and rugged shorelines. The Provence Sunflower Pottery Vase and Provence Lavender Honey Jar draw directly from the colors and textures of his grandparents’ farmhouse—sunburnt terracotta, pale stone walls, and fields humming with bees. Together, these pieces weave his memories of cafés, coastlines, and gardens into a collection that invites people to bring a quiet slice of French everyday life into their own homes.
